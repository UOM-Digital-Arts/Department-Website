Through interdisciplinary and crossmediatic routes, where artistic and scientific research fuse with technology, the MFA in Digital Arts degree caters for a combination of traditional and contemporary studio and lab practices grounded by a contextual and theoretical framework. It promotes a self-directed research approach fostering conceptual articulation within a creative and experimental environment. The curriculum is grounded in a visual arts tradition and is further enriched by opportunities to work between and across disciplines in collaboration with other departments, schools and institutes on campus.

Interested candidates are to contact the Department prior to applying.
